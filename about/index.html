About Me

I'm Hause, a researcher at MIT and Cornell University, and a data scientist at the World Bank. I like to engineer and test solutions to empower people. I also like to play piano. If you find anything interesting here or want to chat about anything, don't hesitate to reach out!

I work with Gordon Pennycook (Cornell University), David Rand (MIT), and Samuel Fraiberger (World Bank Group Development Impact Evaluation Unit). I enjoy integrating ideas from different disciplines and experimenting with computational methods for studying and improving decision making.

During my Ph.D., I worked with Michael Inzlicht (psychologist and social neuroscientist), Cendri Hutcherson (decision neuroscientist), and Mike X Cohen (neuroscientist, writer, and educator). I also worked with Bernardo Blum (economist).

For my Ph.D., I analyzed simulated and real neural time-series data, and tried to figure out how to best use high-dimensional neural data to study how we and/or our brains make decisions.

I'm always looking for new opportunities to apply and improve my skills on experimental methodology, causal inference, data science, and machine learning, time-series analysis, signal processing, and so on (classical and jazz piano playing and composition). I also love building stuff with Svelte and SvelteKit.

Download my academic CV or see LinkedIn if you want drier facts about me. Feel free to get in touch via email or X (@hauselin). To see what I've been up to, check out my projects, Github (@hauselin), or Spotify playlist.
