About Me

I'm Hause, a Ph.D. student at the University of Toronto in Canada. I enjoy working on fun projects, learning new skills, and discussing ideas with everyone. I also like to play piano.

My current advisors are Dr. Michael Inzlicht (social neuroscientist in Toronto), Dr. Cendri Hutcherson (decision neuroscientist in Toronto), and Dr. Mike X Cohen (neuroscientist and writer in The Netherlands). I'm also a research assistant working with Dr. Bernardo Blum (economist in Toronto).

I enjoy exploring and integrating ideas and methods from different disciplines and experimenting with different data science methods for studying human behavior.

For my Ph.D., I analyze simulated and real neural time-series data, and try to figure out how to best use high-dimensional neural data to study how we and/or our brains make decisions.

I'm always looking for new opportunities to apply and improve my skills on experimental methodology, causal inference, data science, and machine learning, time-series analysis, signal processing, and so on (classical and jazz piano playing and composition)...

Download my academic CV if you want drier facts about me. Feel free to get in touch via email or Twitter (@hauselin). To see what I've been up to, check out my projects, my Github (@hauselin), and my Spotify playlist.
